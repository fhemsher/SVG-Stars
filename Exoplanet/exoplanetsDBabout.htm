Exoplanet Data

Host Star Name Stellar name most commonly used in the literature.
HD Name Name of the star as given by the Henry Draper Catalog.
HIP Name Name of the star as given by the Hipparcos Catalog.
Planet Letter Letter assigned to the planetary component of a planetary system.
Discovery Method Method by which the planet was first identified.
Number of Planets in System Number of planets in the planetary system.
Orbital Period (days) Time the planet takes to make a complete orbit around the host star or system.
Orbit Semi-Major Axis (AU) The longest diameter of an elliptic orbit, or for directly imaged planets, the projected separation in the plane of the sky.
Eccentricity Amount by which the orbit of the planet deviates from a perfect circle.
Inclination (deg) Angular distance of the orbital plane from the line of sight.
Planet Mass or M*sin(i) [Jupiter mass] Best planet mass measurement in units of masses of Jupiter. Either Mass, M*sin(i)/sin(i), or M*sin(i). See provenance for source of the measurement.
Planet Mass or M*sin(i) Provenance Provenance of the measurement of the best mass. Options are: Mass, Msin(i)/sin(i), and Msini.
Planet Radius (Jupiter radii) Length of a line segment from the center of the planet to its surface, measured in units of radius of Jupiter.
Planet Density (g/cm**3) Amount of mass per unit of volume of the planet.
TTV Flag Flag indicating if the planet orbit exhibits transit timing variations from another planet in the system (1=yes, 0=no).
Kepler Field Flag Flag indicating if the planetary system signature is present in data taken with the Kepler mission (1=yes, 0=no).
K2 Mission Flag Flag indicating if the planetary system signature is present in data taken with the K2 Mission (1=yes, 0=no).
Number of Notes Number of Notes associated with the planet. View all notes in the Confirmed Planet Overview page.
RA (sexagesimal) Right Ascension of the planetary system in sexagesimal format.
Dec (sexagesimal) Declination of the planetary system in sexagesimal notation.
RA (decimal degrees) Right Ascension of the planetary system in decimal degrees.
Dec (decimal degrees) Declination of the planetary system in decimal degrees.
Distance (pc) Distance to the planetary system in units of parsecs.
Optical Magnitude [mag] Brightness of the host star as measured using the V (Johnson) or the Kepler-band in units of magnitudes.
Optical Magnitude Band Band corresponding to the Optical Magnitude. Options are: V (Johnson) or Kepler-band.
Effective Temperature (K) Temperature of the star as modeled by a black body emitting the same total amount of electromagnetic radiation.
Stellar Mass (solar mass) Amount of matter contained in the star, measured in units of masses of the Sun.
Stellar Radius (solar radii) Length of a line segment from the center of the star to its surface, measured in units of radius of the Sun.
Date of Last Update Date of last update of the planet parameters.
RA (hours) Right Ascension of the planetary system in decimal hours.
Galactic Longitude (deg) Galactic longitude of the planetary system in units of decimal degrees.
Galactic Latitude (deg) Galactic latitude of the planetary system in units of decimal degrees.
Ecliptic Longitude (deg) Ecliptic longitude of the planetary system in units of decimal degrees.
Ecliptic Latitude (deg) Ecliptic latitude of the planetary system in units of decimal degrees.
Parallax (mas) Difference in the angular position of a star as measured at two opposite positions within the Earth's orbit.
RA Proper Motion (mas/yr) Angular change in right ascension over time as seen from the center of mass of the Solar System.
Dec Proper Motion (mas/yr) Angular change in declination over time as seen from the center of mass of the Solar System.
Total Proper Motion (mas/yr) Angular change in position over time as seen from the center of mass of the Solar System.
Radial Velocity (km/sec) Velocity of the star in the direction of the line of sight.
Spectral Type Classification of the star based on their spectral characteristics following the Morgan-Keenan system.
Stellar Surface Gravity Gravitational acceleration experienced at the stellar surface.
Stellar Luminosity [log(solar)] Amount of energy emitted by a star per unit time, measured in units of solar luminosities.
Stellar Density [g/cm**3] Amount of mass per unit of volume of the star.
Stellar Metallicity (dex) Measurement of the metal content of the photosphere of the star as compared to the hydrogen content.
Metallicity Ratio Ratio for the Metallicity Value ([Fe/H] denotes iron abundance, [M/H] refers to a general metal content)
Stellar Age [Gyr] The age of the host star.
Rotational Velocity v*sin(i) [km/s] Rotational velocity at the equator of the star multiplied by the sine of the inclination.
Stellar Activity Index (S-Index) Chromospheric activity as measured by the S-index (ratio of the emission of the H and K Ca lines to that in nearby continuum).
Stellar Activity Log (R'HK) Chromospheric activity as measured by the log(R' HK) index, with is based on the S-index, but excludes the photospheric component in the Ca lines.
x-ray Activity (Lx)" Stellar activity as measured by the total luminosity in X-rays.
SWASP Identifier Name of the star as given by the SuperWASP (Wide Angle Search for Planets) project.
Number of Time Series Number of literature time series available for this star in the NASA Exoplanet Archive
Number of Planet Transit Light Curves Number of literature transit light curves available for this star in the NASA Exoplanet Archive.
Number of General Light Curves Number of Hipparcos light curves available for this star in the NASA Exoplanet Archive.
Number of Radial Velocity Time Series Number of literature radial velocity curves available for this star in the NASA Exoplanet Archive.
