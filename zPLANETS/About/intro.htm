Emerald Map...Communities
Instantly create an online map for any location in the world: Species migration, sensitive habitat, weather tracking, incidence monitoring, status updates... Insert photos, symbols, draw paths, and write descriptions. Update the map as things change. Users can join your community.
How To Begin A Community
1.Zoom into your community on our world map.
2.Give this community a name, and describe it.
3.Provide your email and name.
4.Select the "Begin This Community" button.
This will immediately publish your zoomable map online. Add symbols, photos, and paths, plus write comments on the map. In the future, you can view your community online plus add to, or change it. Your community map will be published, whereby viewers can visit and join your community. You will go to www.emeraldMap.com/Communities/me and this will automatically show your community. Symbols, photos, paths, and descriptions can be added and changed at any time.
To create and visit your communities on your PC, smartphone, and tablet, it is best to use Google's Chrome browser.

Click on the map at where you want to place the center of a circle. A smaller white draggable circle will be shown, used to adjust the desired circle radius. You may also move the circle by dragging it. When the circle is as desired, select the 'finish' button.
Editing Circle - After a circle is finished, it can be moved/changed/resized/removed. Double-Click on the circle you want to change.
Click on the map at where you want to place the upper-left corner of a rectangle. A small draggable circle will appear at the bottom-right corner used to adjust the desired width and height. You can resize/move/rotate the rectangle as desired. Then select 'finish' to save the rectangle to the server.
To rotate the rectangle, toggle the + or - buttons. The default toggled value is 5°. You may select a different toggle value via the drop-down menu.

Editing Rectangle - After a rectangle is finished, it can be rotated/moved/changed/resized/removed. Double-Click on the rectangle you want to change.
Linear and smooth, plus right angled and enclosed paths can be added. Point/Click on the map to community each path point. By selecting the 'pause' button, each point can be moved. Toggle the 'undo' button to remove points sequentially. A path can enclosed, connecting the last point to the first point. Select 'finish' to save the path to the server. (To finish, you may also use the keyboard [F] key.)
Types of Paths
As the path segments are being drawn, you may pause it and then drag its points. After finished dragging the points, re-select the pause/restart button to continue.

Right Angled L: - The path segments are automatically aligned to maintain 90° between each segment.

Long Paths: Use Keyboard Arrows
When a path is to extend beyond the current map view area, use the keyboard arrows to navigate the map. This will pan the map in the direction of the arrow. By continuing to click points, you will extend the path in the desired direction.

Comments
Whan an element is created, it can include a comment. Therefore when the cursor moves over that element, it shows the associated comment.
When an element is double-clicked for changes, the comment can be changed or removed.


Important: Map Zoom Level @
The zoom level of the map is shown when adding icons, photos, paths, and description text. To present consistent sizes, it is important to be aware of this zoom value. The size selected for these elements apply only to the zoom level where they are created: As the map is zoomed in/out they become larger/smaller. (The size selected for symbols, however, are maintained at all zoom levels.)
When an element is double-clicked for changes, the map automatically zooms to the level which the element was created.


Return To Original View
As the original boundries of your community are zoomed out, the boundries are highlighted with a transparent background color.
To return the map to the original view of the community boundries, click on the name of the community.

Note on Automatic Login:
Rather than manually logging in to your community, cookies provide automatic login. They are used to remember the communities you own and are a friend. Your last visited community will be the one that automatically logs in on your next visit. Your other communities are listed in the drop-down menu.

If you change computers or browsers the cookies will be lost. However they will be refeshed as you login to the communities you own or are a user. Therefore you should keep a record your communities ID's.
